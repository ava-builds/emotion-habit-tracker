# UX Research Insights

## Survey Overview

- Total responses: 14
- Target users:
  - Students
  - Working professionals
  - People balancing study and work

---

# Key Findings

## 1. Emotional stress is common

Most participants reported feeling stressed or emotionally overwhelmed occasionally or often.

Insight:
Users may need a lightweight emotional reflection tool that does not create additional pressure.

---

## 2. Many users stopped using tracking apps

Main reasons:
- Forgot to open the app regularly
- Felt judged or pressured
- Apps felt cold or clinical
- Tracking took too much effort

Insight:
The app should feel emotionally safe, soft, and low-pressure.

---

## 3. Users value simplicity

Most users only want to spend:
- 1–2 minutes
or
- 3–5 minutes

on emotional check-ins.

Insight:
Daily interaction must be extremely lightweight.

---

## 4. Visual design matters a lot

Average visual importance rating:
4.21 / 5

Insight:
Users emotionally connect with visual atmosphere and comfort.

---

## 5. Users want meaningful insights

Popular requested features:
- Monthly mood calendar
- Habit streak tracking
- Emotional pattern insights

Insight:
Users want reflection and understanding, not only data collection.

---

## 6. Emotional safety is important

Some users mentioned:
- wanting apps that feel understanding
- wanting non-judgmental experiences
- wanting emotional context instead of robotic tracking

Insight:
The app should feel human-centered rather than analytical.

---

# Design Direction Based on Research

Based on survey responses, the app should:

- reduce emotional pressure
- avoid overwhelming interfaces
- prioritize calm visual design
- provide gentle emotional insights
- support reflection without judgment
- minimize daily effort
- create emotional comfort instead of productivity stress

---

# Initial Product Direction

The app will focus on:
- calm emotional check-ins
- habit + emotion relationship tracking
- soft visual journaling
- low-effort interaction
- emotional pattern awareness
- optional reflective insights
